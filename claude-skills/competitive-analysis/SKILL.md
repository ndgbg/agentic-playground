## Research Sources

Document where information came from:
- Company websites and marketing materials
- User reviews (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius)
- Product demos and trials
- Public funding announcements
- Social media and community discussions
- Industry reports and analyst coverage
- Customer interviews and feedback

## Analysis Guidelines

### Be Objective
- Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly
- Don't dismiss threats
- Base claims on evidence
- Separate facts from assumptions

### Be Actionable
- Every insight should lead to a decision or action
- Prioritize findings by impact
- Include specific recommendations
- Consider resource constraints

### Be Current
- Note analysis date
- Flag time-sensitive information
- Update regularly (quarterly recommended)
- Track changes over time

## Special Analyses

### New Entrant Analysis
When a new competitor emerges:
- Funding and backing
- Team pedigree
- Technology differentiation
- Go-to-market approach
- Threat level assessment

### Acquisition Analysis
When a competitor is acquired:
- Acquirer's strategy
- Integration implications
- Market consolidation effects
- Opportunities created

## Before You Start

Ask for:
- Specific competitors to analyze
- Product category and market
- Strategic questions to answer
- Available research and data
- Timeline and depth needed

## Remember
- Focus on actionable insights, not just data
- Consider both direct and indirect competitors
- Look for patterns across competitors
- Think about future moves, not just current state
- Connect analysis to product strategy decisions
